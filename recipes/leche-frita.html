Leche Frita

Description

Originating in Spain, this delectable treat is literally fried milk!!! Soft pudding like interior with a slight crunch on the outside, you will love this sweet treat!

Prep time and chilling the milk may take a while, so patience is both key and a virtue to making this dish.

Inspired by Spain on a Fork

Ingredients

  • 4 cups milk
  • 1 cup corn starch
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon powder
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 4 lemon peels
  • 1/2 lemon
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 cup sunflower oil

Steps For Milk Mixture

  1. Add 3 cups of milk into a sautee pan, add 1 cinnamon stick, 4 lemon peels and heat with medium heat until light boil, then turn off heat
  2. While milk is infusing, add a cup of milk to a separate bowl, slowly add 1/2 cup of corn starch while whisking together
  3. Grease a 10 by 6 inch casserole dish with 1 tsp of sunflower oil, covering the inside of the dish completely.
  4. After infusing the milk mixture for 20 minutes, remove lemon peels and cinnamon stick, heat with medium heat, pour in the bowl with the corstarch and milk mixture and whisk it together, at the same time adding 1/2 cup granulated sugar while continuing to whisk until all sugar is added. Continue whisking for 20-25 minutes or until texture is thick and creamy.
  5. Transfer the thickened mixture to the casserole dish and allow to cool, then cover with saran wrap. Put in fridge for at least 2 hour to firm and solidify.

Steps For Frying Leche Frita

  1. Remove the casserole dish and place a cutting board over the dish and invert, trying not to break the mixture. Cut the milk mixture into 1 1/2 inch by 1 1/2 squares.
  2. Add 1/2 cup corn starch to one bowl, then crack 2 eggs into another bowl and beat the eggs
  3. Dust milk squares in corn starch, then in egg wash, then add to a plate covered with parchment paper. Repeat until all squares are coveren in corn starch and egg wash.
  4. Heat up a small frying pan to medium heat and add 1/2 cup of of sunflower oil to pan. After 4 minutes, start adding the coated squares into pan in a single layer, making sure to cook in small batchs, cook for 45-60 secons per plate, then transfer them to plate with paper towels.
  5. Finally, dust the cooked squares in a bowl with a mixture of 1/4 cup of granulated sugar + 1/2 tsp cinnamon powder.
  6. Add to serving dish with a sprinkle of lemon zest. ENJOY!!
